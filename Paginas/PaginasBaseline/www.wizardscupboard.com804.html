Homes for MM17 Rares and Mythics

By Byron King
@DoneTheMath
 
Modern Masters 2017 comes stuffed with great reprints for the format.  If there has ever been a push for a cheap Modern format, the time is now.  Below, I’ve looked at all of the rares and mythics in Modern Masters 2017 and identified all of their current homes.  If you’re looking for ideas of what to do with your newfound riches, you’ve come to the right place.  If you’re looking for people to trade your extra cards to, this might help you narrow down your targets.
 
I’m going to use the list of named decks on mtgtop8.com that break 1% of the format.  That isn’t a complete list, but it comes close enough for our needs.  That list of decks is:

Modern Homes: Hatebears (3%)
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
Blade Splicer wasn’t ever a difficult card to find for the Hatebear deck, but a reprint will prevent the price from creeping up for a long time.  It has the chance to show up in the Bant Spirits or Creatures Toolbox decks, but it isn’t a staple in any of those decks.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: Medium
Casual Appeal: Medium
A lack of Brainstorm and Sensei’s Divining Top keep the Miracles mechanic out of the format.  No Modern decks are really interested in this effect.

Modern Homes: Creatures Toolbox
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
Linvala, Keeper of Silence is more of a sideboard card than a deck staple.  Any deck with Chord of Calling usually has a copy floating around somewhere for the mirror.  It isn’t common anywhere else.  Scarcity has been driving the price of Linvala for years now, and it should be priced far more reasonably from now on.

Modern Homes: Death’s Shadow (7%), Martyr Life (1%), Zoo (2%)
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
Martyr Life is the only deck that actually plays up to a full set of Ranger of Eos.  Death’s Shadow uses it for a sideboard strategy against grindy decks, and Zoo has almost completely dropped it at this point.

Modern Homes: Creature Toolbox (3%), Hatebears (3%), U/W/x Midrange (2%)
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Medium
Restoration Angel is versatile enough that it has several homes.  The various Creature Toolbox decks use it as a combo with Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker.  Hatebears use it as a form of protection and for small amounts of value.  The various Azorius midrange decks use it as a finisher and for value off of Snapcaster Mage and Wall of Omens.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: None (for completion sake, it’s probably medium)
Séance has made its mark on MTG history as the linchpin for a handful of weird stories.  The weirdest has to be a Reddit user trying to hire a player to play 4x Séance at a Pro Tour.  Nobody actually wants to play this card competitively.

Modern Homes: Eldrazi Aggro (9%), Abzan (6%), Creature Toolbox (3%), Hatebears (3%), Bant CoCo (2%), Nahiri Control (2%), U/W Control (2%), U/W/x Midrange (2%), Zoo (2%), Hexproof (1%)
Legacy Appeal: Low
Casual Appeal: Low
Possibly the single most popular sideboard card, this is the best answer to Affinity in the format.  If a deck has white cards and doesn’t rely on artifacts, Stony Silence is probably in the sideboard.

Modern Homes: Nahiri Control (2%)
Legacy Appeal: Medium
Casual Appeal: Medium
Nahiri Control decks occasionally play Terminus, but it isn’t necessary for the deck.  Like Entreat the Angels, the Miracle mechanic is hard to use in Modern.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Medium
Limited rare, no use in Modern.

Modern Homes: UrzaTron (6%)
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: High
Claiming that Cyclonic Rift goes in UrzaTron is somewhat of a stretch, since only the mono blue version runs it.  It isn’t even a lock to be in that deck.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: High
Commander all-star, Modern dud.

Modern Homes: UrzaTron (6%), Storm (2%), U/W Control (2%)
Legacy Appeal: Low
Casual Appeal: Low
Mono blue Tron and U/W Control occasionally play Gifts Ungiven with an Unburial Rites package.  Storm uses Gifts Ungiven as a key card to combo with Baral or Goblin Electromancer.  Storm is the biggest home for Gifts Ungiven in the current Modern format.

Modern Homes: Bant CoCo (2%), Merfolk (2%)
Legacy Appeal: Low
Casual Appeal: High
The Bant lists are not refined, so Phantasmal Image isn’t a guaranteed inclusion in all the lists.  The combination with Drogskol Captain is nifty enough to warrant consideration.  Merfolk sometimes plays a few Phantasmal Images to complement the Lords.

Modern Homes: Grixis Control (5%), Nahiri Control (2%), U/W Control (2%), U/W/x Midrange (2%), Blue Moon (1%), Faeries (1%), U/R Prowess (1%)
Legacy Appeal: Medium
Casual Appeal: Medium
If a Modern deck plays blue and isn’t a dedicated combo deck, Snapcaster Mage will be present in some number.  This is the premier blue card in Modern.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Medium
The Modern Taking Turns deck actually runs a set of Temporal Mastery, but that deck didn’t make the list on Mtgtop8.com.  Nothing else is interested in this card.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: Low
Casual Appeal: Medium
Venser is just a little too slow for Modern.  It is actually more popular in Legacy, where the synergy with Karakas really shines.

Modern Homes: Abzan (6%), Grixis Control (5%), Jund (5%), Faeries (1%), Golgari Rock (1%)
Legacy Appeal: Low
Casual Appeal: High
Damnation shows up as a 1x or 2x in sideboards of various black decks.  It isn’t a defining card in any premier Modern deck.

Modern Homes: Death’s Shadow (7%)
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
Clearly the Death’s Shadow deck is going to play a set of Death’s Shadow.  This is quite possibly the best Modern deck right now, so it will be popular even if it only has one home.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
I suppose there are some people that want to relive Mono Black Devotion in Commander or other casual formats.  Nobody else is interested in Desecration Demon.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
There are some mill decks that consider Extractor Demon, but it hasn’t made an appearance in several years now.

Modern Homes: Goryo’s Vengeance (2%)
Legacy Appeal: High
Casual Appeal: Low
If a Modern deck is capable of cheating a giant monster into play, Griselbrand is the best payoff card after Emrakul, the Aeons Torn.  There are some Expertise decks in the fringes of Modern, but Goryo’s Vengeance is the only deck with a pedigree that runs the giant Necropotence.

Modern Homes: Death’s Shadow (7%), Abzan (6%), Jund (5%), Faeries (1%), Golgari Rock (1%)
Legacy Appeal: High
Casual Appeal: Medium
The most popular Planeswalker in Modern, Liliana of the Veil is a staple in the Jund, Abzan, and Golgari Rock decks.  Death’s Shadow plays a mix of Liliana Planeswalker cards, and Faeries occasionally uses the card.  Liliana is one of the best cards in the set, as there are multiple popular decks that need access to her.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Medium
Modern dud.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Medium
See Mind Shatter.

Modern Homes: Affinity (6%), Goryo’s Vengeance (2%), Nahiri Control (2%), Storm (2%), Living End (1%), Ponza (Land Destruction) (1%), Scred Red (1%)
Legacy Appeal: Medium
Casual Appeal: Low
Blood Moon probably sits at the top of the pile for cards that have “ruined” a game of Magic.  Because of the free win potential, Blood Moon is a staple a few decks and a sideboard card in several others.

Modern Homes: Ponza (Land Destruction) (1%)
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Medium
Bonfire of the Damned has some of the same problems as Terminus and Entreat the Angels.  The Ponza deck ramps hard enough that the regular cost of Bonfire of the Damned isn’t completely embarrassing, so it is included more often than not.

Modern Homes: Burn (6%), Zoo (2%), U/R Prowess (1%)
Legacy Appeal: Medium
Casual Appeal: Low
Goblin Guide is THE iconic card in the Burn deck.  Zoo usually plays it, but it isn’t a guarantee.  U/R Prowess can play it, but it isn’t that common.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: Low
Casual Appeal: Medium
This cube all-star doesn’t quite make the cut in Modern.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
I suppose any of the midrange red decks could play Mizzium Mortars, but Flame Slash usually knocks it out.  The Overload cost is a little too steep for Modern.

Modern Homes: Storm (2%)
Legacy Appeal: Low
Casual Appeal: Low
Past in Flames is the Yawgmoth’s Will replacement for Modern Storm, and it is vital for the current builds.  No other deck is interested in it.

Modern Homes: Storm (2%)
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
It’s probably generous to count Storm as a deck that plays Pyromancer’s Ascension, since the current versions have all cut the card.  There are some Jeskai Ascendancy decks that run Pyromancer’s Ascension, but that is not a common deck.

Modern Homes: Creature Toolbox (3%)
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: High
Zealous Conscripts is a combo with Kiki-Jiki, the Mirror Breaker, so there are some of the Creature Toolbox decks that run it.  It is a little too expensive for other decks to play.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: Medium
Casual Appeal: High
Elves could play Craterhoof Behemoth, but Ezuri, Renegade Leader has that role covered and plays better with Collected Company.

Modern Homes: Valakut (5%), Ponza (Land Destruction) (1%)
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Medium
While not a common card in any deck, the various ramp decks in Modern occasionally play the card. It is a niche sideboard card for ramp mirrors that has some play against unexpected decks.

Modern Homes: Abzan (6%), Jund (5%), Creature Toolbox (3%), Bant CoCo (2%), Zoo (2%), Elves (1%), Golgari Rock (1%)
Legacy Appeal: Medium
Casual Appeal: Medium
Scavenging Ooze is a staple in Abzan and Jund.  It compliments Tarmogoyf as an additional cheap threat, and it has utility against various graveyard decks.  Any deck with Chord of Calling or Collected Company usually has a copy of Scavenging Ooze floating around somewhere in the 75.

Modern Homes: Valakut (5%)
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Medium
Once again, I’m being generous including Summoning Trap in the Valakut decks.  It is an occasional sideboard card, but it is far from being a staple.  There is a very fringe deck based around Windbrisk Heights that sometimes includes Summoning Trap.

Modern Homes: Death’s Shadow (7%), Abzan (6%), Jund (5%), Hatebears (3%), Bant CoCo (2%), Zoo (2%), Golgari Rock (1%)
Legacy Appeal: High
Casual Appeal: Medium
If your deck needs an efficient, big, dumb creature, Tarmogoyf has your back.  If a deck only wants to devote a few cards to actually winning a game, Tarmogoyf is a good fit for the job.  Normally, it is a two mana 4/5, but the Death’s Shadow deck can reach 7/8 without help from an opponent.  Zoo decks often include Tarmogoyf for the stats, but it isn’t much better than several other options there.

Modern Homes: Creatures Toolbox (3%)
Legacy Appeal: Low
Casual Appeal: Medium
Shenanigans with Restoration Angel will keep Thragtusk on the fringes of Modern.  It is a little too expensive for most other decks, but I’m sure there are some ramp decks (Valakut, Ponza) that might consider it for a sideboard option.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
I suppose there is a universe where Creature Toolbox decks might consider Ulvenwald Tracker, but this isn’t that universe.

Modern Homes: Eldrazi Aggro (9%)
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: High
Walking Ballista recently brought Basilisk Collar into Modern in the EldraziTron deck.  It was a Standard combo with Cunning Sparkmage, but the Creature Toolbox decks haven’t used that in years.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
Hate for Splinter Twin?  This kind of effect hasn’t seen play in Modern in about a year.

Modern Homes: Lots
Legacy Appeal: High
Casual Appeal: Low
There are a ton of decks that play Grafdigger’s Cage.  It is good against graveyard decks, Snapcaster Mage decks, and Chord of Calling decks, so almost every other deck runs copies to fight those decks.  They synergy with Ancient Stirrings is an added bonus.

Modern Homes: Death’s Shadow (7%), Abzan (6%), Jund (5%), Creature Toolbox (3%), Dredge (3%), Elves (1%), Golgari Rock (1%), Lantern Control (1%)
Legacy Appeal: High
Casual Appeal: Low
Pretty much all the Thoughtseize decks that have access to green play some number of Abrupt Decay.  Dredge uses Abrupt Decay out of the board as an answer to opposing hate cards.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
The only 5/5 Wurm tokens in Modern come from Worldspine Wurm.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Medium
Costs a little too much for Modern.

Modern Homes: Scred Red (1%)
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Medium
Boros Reckoner is a little too mana intensive for any deck besides Scred Red.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
A relic of Standard Jund, I don’t think Broodmate Dragon has ever made the cut in Modern.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: High
I can respect someone for trying, but Cruel Ultimatum is a little too expensive for Modern.

Modern Homes: Creature Toolbox (3%)
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Medium
A few of the Naya Creature Toolbox decks play Domri Rade in the sideboard, but it isn’t a common inclusion.  Nahiri, the Harbringer has pretty much crushed Domri Rade’s sliver of Modern playability.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Medium
Cute, but no.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
Falkenrath Aristocrat isn’t an embarrassing finisher for a Jund Zoo deck.  That deck probably doesn’t exist in the face of Death’s Shadow.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
There have been some rumblings about Fiery Justice recently as a hate card for Death’s Shadow.  It, Grove of the Burnwillows, and Kavu Predator can make a nice package, but there aren’t any decks that currently use it.  The fact that Punishing Fire is banned doesn’t help.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
Not even close to Modern playable.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
More Commander legends.

Modern Homes: Jund (5%)
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
Olivia Voldaren shows up in some Jund decks in the old Bloodbraid Elf slot.  Kalitas, Traitor of Ghet has almost pushed Olivia Voldaren out of Modern, but it still makes an occasional appearance.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
A little too expensive for Modern.

Modern Homes: U/W Control (2%)
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Medium
Sphinx’s Revelation is a little too expensive for most decks, but there are a few hardcore control decks that manage to squeeze in a couple copies.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
I don’t even know what deck would want this kind of effect.

Modern Homes: Abzan (6%), Creature Toolbox (3%), Hatebears (3%),
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
Abzan and Hatebears uses Voice of Resurgence as an efficient threat that helps put the squeeze on opponents.  The Creature Toolbox decks use it for the same reason, but they are better at cashing it in for the Elemental Token when they need it.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
See Stoic Angel.

Modern Homes: None
Legacy Appeal: None
Casual Appeal: Low
There is an Esper Zur the Enchanter deck, but it isn’t that popular.  That deck uses Zur to tutor for Detention Sphere, Steel of the Godhead, and Spreading Seas.

Modern Homes: Nahiri Control (2%), U/W/x Midrange (2%), Zoo (2%)
Legacy Appeal: High
Casual Appeal: High
There are plenty of decks that use Arid Mesa for either red or white lands.  The decks I listed are the decks that specifically need Arid Mesa because they include both colors.

Legacy Appeal: High

Any deck that has a tribal theme that doesn’t rely heavily on specific lands is going to play some number of Cavern of Souls.  Eldrazi Aggro is specifically interested since it can produce <> mana for Eldrazi, even if that means the creature is counterable.

There are plenty of decks that use Misty Rainforest for either blue or green lands.  The decks I listed are the decks that specifically need Misty Rainforest because they include both colors.
