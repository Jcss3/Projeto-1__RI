Ten Magic Origins Cards that will Change EDH

Ten Magic Origins Cards that will Change EDH

by Craig Sidorowicz

Legendary creatures that turn into Planeswalkers!? How exciting for EDH…at first glance.  The “TransWalkers,” as I call them, are not exactly top tier mono-color Commander material.  Instead, as I will explain, they should be viewed as role-players (at best) in existing deck archetypes.  There are however, several cards that will have an impact on our tables, for better or for worse.  I present to you, in no particular order: the ten Magic Origins cards that will change EDH.

Artifact

Sword of the Animist

My last two articles focused heavily on mana in EDH, especially the concepts of land and resource acquisition during the building stages of the game.  I explained that it’s rather easy to ramp and fix mana if playing green, but it can be a struggle if not.  Sword of the Animist will provide another avenue for non-green players to ramp and fix basic lands at a reasonable cost.  It should by no means supplant tier one mana rocks or Solemn Simulacrum, but it will function as a more aggressive, and likely better version of Journeyer’s kite.

White:

Tragic Arrogance

At first, I glanced over the “nonland” stipulation and thought that this was an extremely broken version of the already broken Cataclysm.  Upon rereading, I discovered that we are not as screwed as I originally thought, but it still presents the caster with a cornucopia of card advantage.  I especially like the card design,  because it rewards players for recognizing and choosing the “least threatening” of their opponent’s permanents, so player experience and skill comes in handy with this one.  As many white cards in MTG tend to do, It rewards players who keep their board “humble” with a few high quality permanents, and as the card name suggests, punishes those who overextend.

Starfield of Nyx

“Fun,” headache-inducing interactions come to mind with cards such as Heliod, Dictate of Heliod, Opalescence, Enchanted Evening, Enduring Ideal,  Humility, Sterling Grove, Uril the Miststalker, and Zur the Enchanter just to name a few.  Enchantment heavy, defensive “pillow fort” decks (like Oloro Esper Control) rejoice.

Blue

Day’s Undoing

It’s Timetwister, but you have to Time Stop yourself.  This comes with the side effect that it must exile itself upon resolution, which prevents players from getting too crazy with recursion shenanigans.  It’s still a great tool for disruptive Nekusar decks, alongside it’s menacing lineup of Teferi’s Puzzle Boxes, Wheels of Fortune, Underworld Dreams, and Fate Unravelers.  It’s also a great tool for blue players to disrupt graveyard-based decks without the “Power Nine” Price tag.

Displacement Wave

Pernicious Deed meets Cyclonic Rift.  While I don’t think it’s as powerful as either of the aforementioned cards because it’s a sorcery,  I believe it will be another good tool for control decks with large creatures (such as my Mimeoplasm Reanimator deck) or a good way to wipe out tokens for only two blue mana.  It can also be used to generate mana using cards like Mana Crypt, Mana Vault, Sol Ring, and Grim Monolith

Green

The Great Aurora

Cyclonic Rift…Winds of Change…Warp World?  The Great Aurora firmly draws a line in the sand as: “green’s most chaotic card.”  Unfortunately, this kind of chaos is not something that green often wants to do, especially after it’s committed a sizable number of lands and permanents onto the battlefield.  I’d still love to see this happen, and I’m sure I will…as will a number of masochistically benevolent Phelddagrif players.

Evolutionary Leap

I see this as the most powerful card in Magic Origins, and I predict that it will see play in several formats.  It’s name, casting cost and activation cost immediately brought to mind Survival of the Fittest.  One of the drawbacks to having a board full of green creatures (and likely an empty hand) is that they are extremely vulnerable to board wipes.  Now, you can sacrifice as many creatures as you can pay for to refill your hand next turn.  In addition to providing a failsafe measure, it also provides a sacrifice outlet synergy, a way to gain value from a dying chump blocker, or can even be used to dig for creatures in the late game by casting, and then sacrificing the creature you dug for using the card’s ability.

Woodland Bellower

Make no mistake, this card is powerful, and probably my second favorite in the set.  It interacts with over 800 creatures, and will continue to grow in power as new, non legendary green cards are printed.  In addition to it’s potential to find most elves, it can also be used to look for the following EDH-relevant non-legendary green creatures:

Vexing Shusher

 My favorite is Eternal Witness (by far), and I’d be very fond of finding Dauntless Escort, Knight of the Reliquary, Vexing Shusher, Harmonic Sliver, and Scavenging Ooze.

 Black

Languish

I was hard-pressed to find a relevant black and red cards, but I feel that Languish could works nicely as another copy of Toxic Deluge.  Unless I was playing Mono-Black or Urborg was on the table, I’d prefer this card to Mutilate as well, since it doesn’t require the caster to have swamps.

Red

Chandra’s Ignition

This was the most interesting red card in the set for EDH, and I’m sure that Uril the Miststalker or Iroas/Tajic decks with a lot of indestructibility would enjoy having another potentially one-sided board clear like Blasphemous Act.  Did anyone else notice that this is very similar to the Hearthstone card: Shadowflame?

Honorable Mentions:

The TransWalkers:

Don’t get me wrong…I love the design and story behind these cards, and I also realize that they couldn’t make them extremely broken.  Wizards is telling the origin story of their namesake Planeswalkers, and it wouldn’t make story-sense for these versions to be extremely powerful, ala “The Mind Sculptor” or “Of the Veil,” since they are essentially “Planeswalker Newbies.”  Standard and Tiny Leaders seem to be good homes for these, but again, I’m not sure they make the cut in EDH.

Liliana seems to be the most promising of the bunch, with a relatively easy trigger to transform her, the 2/2 zombie body to protect her, as well as a +2 that affects the board and feeds her -X ability.  Will I preorder and play with her (or any of the TransWalkers) immediately?  In all likelihood no.  I may pick her up once the novelty and hype dies down, as well as her card price.  As a general, she would have quite a bit of competition: Balthor the Defiled, Chainer Dementia Master, Mikaeus, and Ob Nixilis come to mind.  Oh yeah.. the -8 is Kel Thuzad from Hearthstone.  Illuminati reference, anyone?!!

Nissa is also a promising team player, but her pre-spark form is little more than a Borderland Ranger that we hope survives until our land count reaches seven.  Sure, we can re-cast her from the general zone, but to what end?  She has major competition from Ezuri, Renegade Leader, as well as Freyalise, Llanowar’s Fury.  As for her card drawing mechanic…wouldn’t we rather play Mind’s Eye or Staff of Nin?  They require a lot less effort!

Gideon/Kytheon exists as a legendary Savannah Lion (Or Isamaru, Hound of Konda) with an interesting indestructible ability, and might be the easiest general to build a deck around.  I envision him at his best in an equipment and enchantment-packed 21 commander damage deck.  In fact, I’d almost rather not flip him, as his “infant planeswalker” mode is just as lackluster in EDH as his “grown up” counterpart: Gideon Jura.

Jace’s looting ability also ensures that he will flip quickly, and his -3 ability is quite interesting.  The problem is, his +1 is pretty ineffectual, and will likely not be enough to keep someone at the table from nonchalantly plucking him off of the table.  That said, he is so under the radar that many players might not bother him.  I’d be willing to try him out in a grixis reanimator list, or a spell-based deck like Jeleva.

Chandra, Chandra, Chandra… I’m not sure what to say about this version of her, but suffice to say her only ability with EDH potential is her -7 “set everyone on fire” ability.  It’s flavorful and original to give other players emblems, but I don’t think it’s relevant enough in a 40-life format.

 

Other Honorable Mentions:

These are cards that might make the cut in EDH, but didn’t make the top ten list:

Avaracious Dragon

You greedy, greedy dragon!  Red always wants to draw more cards, and if they can use them “right now” then they benefit from this dragon’s ability.  I find myself really wishing it was a 5/5 though…

Dwynen, Gilt-Leaf Daen

She is another nice elf-lord, but to be a commander of elves, one must pass the Ezuri test.  Unfortunately she does not, and it’s a pity she doesn’t cost 1GG.

Hixus, Prison Warden

He’s pretty cool as a “gotcha!” card, but not as a general, because savvy opponents can play around him when they know he is there.

Kothophed Soul-Hoarder

He’s pretty interesting, but I bet he will kill more than a few players who dare to play him!

Pia and Kiran Nalaar

Chandra’s Parents seem like cool people, and cool people like to play with red spells and artifacts.  I think they would work well in a Daretti, Scrap Savant deck, or perhaps an artifact heavy deck of their own.

Harbinger of the Tides

While tribal merfolk isn’t much of a thing around my tables, I like cards that have flash.  This is a great tempo card, and can turn the tables on someone’s big attacker, or it can be used to save someone from 21 commander damage.  He has potential in a wizard deck.

Mizzium Meddler

Everyone loves (or hates) a Willbender or a Spellskite!  I also think that any creature with flash deserves a second look.

Nissa’s Pilgrimage

Nissa’s Pilgrimage is to Kodama’s reach what Myriad Landscape is to Krosan Verge.  I like that it has a possibility to provide a third land if the spell mastery conditions are met.

Dark Dabbling

Another decent spell mastery card, it can provide a nice dauntless escort effect for your team.  I also like the art that features a mischievous looking “heretical healer” Liliana getting busy learning the secrets of the green ooze…

Elemental Bond

I’m always willing to consider Green card draw!

Shadows of the Past

Viscera Seer has taught me that Scry 1 is great, and I’ve also learned that the more cards I see in an EDH came, the more fun I often have.  Paying five to drain the table for 2x(number of opponents) may come in handy as well if you are like me, and enjoy using your life total as a resource.

Alhammaret’s Archive

Silly lifegain decks will love this card, and Consecrated Sphinx players will giggle with collusion-riddled amusement!  Quite nice with Sphinx’s Revelation as well.  Oh I get it now.. Alhammaret is the king of Sphinxes.  Very clever!  Wouldn’t it be interesting if we could choose legendary lands or legendary artifacts like this in a secondary “general’s lair/equipment” zone?  It would be an interesting house rule to try out.  Dibs on Umezawa’s Jitte!

Helm of the Gods

This has potential to be a decent finisher in enchantment-heavy decks such as Zur, Uril, or Esper control.

Pyromancer’s Goggles

This is quite a flavorful card, and potentially very powerful when used with Wheel of Fortune.

Meteorite: This is just here for the awesome art, flavorful concept,and witty “Roswaterian” flavor text.  if it cost 2 it’d be broken, if it cost 3 it would be useful, but 5 is way too much!

So… do you agree with my assertions?  Did I leave anything out or miss something relevant?  If so, please feel free to let me know via comments or email.  I am always open to suggestions and learning something new.  I hope that this set review gave you an insight into how I evaluate cards in a new set.  Let me conclude by putting my money where my mouth is, and giving you a list of what I preordered from The Wizard’s Cupboard:

2x Dark Dabbling

2x Displacement Wave

4x Evolutionary Leap

1x Languish

2x Nissa’s Pilgrimage

1x Pia and Kiran Nalaar

1x Shadows of the Past

1x Starfield of Nyx

1x Sword of the Animist

2x Tragic Arrogance

2x Woodland Bellower

All of these cards have potential homes in my current decklists.  Here are a few I am saving for later, for when I suspect the market price will come down:

 1x Liliana

1x Jace

1x Kytheon

1x Nissa

1x Day’s Undoing

Of course, I could be wrong and have to pay more for these later, but then again, I don’t really have the desire to put these cards in any of my current decks.  I see it as a risk worth taking for now!

Take care and enjoy,

-Craig
