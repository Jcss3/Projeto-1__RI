MTG Edition Guide

 

ABUGames.com Guide to determine what edition a Magic the Gathering card is from
 

If you would like to know how to tell what set a particular Magic card is from, this web page will show you. Also, this page will answer the age old question - "How do I tell the difference between Revised / 3rd Edition and Unlimited?"

(click on the card pictures below for a larger image)

 
 
Most Magic the Gathering cards have a unique expansion symbol on the right side in about the middle of the card. Each of these symbols represents a certain set (Sets are also referred to as Editions or Releases). The on the card below denotes that this card is from the Champions of Kamigawa set.

The early Base / Core Sets did not have expansion symbols and are more difficult to determine which set they are from. There are also Specialty Sets that often include reprints that use expansion symbols from previous sets which is very confusing. So unfortunately just knowing the expansion symbols doesn't gaurantee a card is from a perticular set. The most common source of errors in missidentifing what set a card is from are cards from Chronicles as these look very similar to Arabian Nights, Antiquities and Legends with those expansion symbols only they are white bordered.

Promo cards are usually special versions of existing cards. They often have an alternate expansion symbol. ABU usually has every promo listed with pictures in the promo section. If you are not sure if a card is a promo, check the Promo section.

Below is a list of all Magic The Gathering sets along with the information you can use to identify what set a card is from.
 
 


Base or Core Sets
 

 

Alpha
(Singles - Sets - Packs)
More rounded corners than any other Magic cards
Black bordered
No expansion symbol
No copyright date
 

Beta
(Singles - Sets - Packs)
Near identical to Alpha except they have regular rounded corners
Black bordered
No expansion symbol
No copyright date
 

Unlimited
(Singles - Sets - Packs)
At first Unlimited and Revised / 3rd Edition cards look the same, but they have some very unique differences. Once you see them, its quite easy to tell them apart.
White bordered
No expansion symbol
No copyright date
Title text is more left justified than Revised / 3rd Edition
Inside border is double beveled
Ink is generally darker than Revised / 3rd Edition
 

Revised / 3rd Edition
(Singles - Sets - Packs)
White bordered
No expansion symbol
No copyright date
Title text is more right justified than Unlimited
Inside border is single beveled
Ink is generally lighter than Unlimited
