Cruising for a Bruising

Khans of Tarkir added a completely new dimension to legacy, just in time for Grand Prix New Jersey. Treasure Cruise takes the cantrip-dense delver decks and pushes them into the stratosphere. All the different color combinations of delver decks can play treasure cruise, but U/R has been taking home the trophies.

The stock U/R delver list
12 Creatures
4 Delver of Secrets
4 Monastery Swiftspear
4 Young Pyromancer

31 Instants/Sorceries
4 Gitaxian Probe
4 Brainstorm
4 Ponder
4 Lightning Bolt
2 Forked Bolt
1 Pyroblast
4 Daze
4 Force of Will
4 Treasure Cruise

17 Lands
4 Volcanic Islands
2 Islands
1 Mountain
4 Scalding Tarn
5 (any blue fetch land)
1 (any red fetch land)

Delver of Secrets – An extremely efficient creature. It requires a high number of spells, but that’s already what the deck wanted to do
Monastery Swiftspear – This is the other Khans addition to the deck. There are turns where this is no better than a Raging Goblin, and then it does 3-4 damage in one turn. It is less consistent than Goblin Guide, but it can be more explosive.
Young Pyromancer – This card is capable of creating an army with a decklist over 50% spells. It can easily take over a game if left unchecked.

Gitaxian Probe – This card synergizes ridiculously well with Young Pyromancer and Monastery Swiftspear. The knowledge gained from the opponent’s hand is also very valuable. It is important to remember that the card can be paid for with mana, as some matches will come down to a race.
Brainstorm – The best spell in legacy
Ponder – Not as good as brainstorm, but keeps the spell count high and supplements the low land count.
Lightning Bolt – It’s lightning bolt. This might be the best reason to play red.
Forked Bolt – This does less damage than the other options in red, but the versatility is strong. It shines against Elves and Death and Taxes.
Chain Lightning – The extra point of damage over Forked Bolt is mostly for racing. Adding up to 20 is easier when each spell does 3 damage instead of 2
Pyroblast – The list that won Legacy Champs cut the single Chain Lightning for a Pyroblast. Having maindeck counterspells for Treasure Cruise can be game winning, but the expense is real. It certainly is awkward in the wrong matchups, but it can always target a non-blue permanent just to fuel delve.
Daze – Free counterspells are always strong, particularly with Young Pyromancer. That said, this is not an optimal shell for Daze. Stifle and Wasteland keep Daze relevant for longer periods in a game, and this deck forgoes both of those cards.
Force of Will – This is the countermeasure for decks that want to win unreasonably fast. Decks like Belcher, Storm, and Reanimator
Treasure Cruise – This is the new Ancestral Recall. The ability to draw 3 in the midgame is the driving force behind this deck, as previous Delver strategies could only gain advantage through tempo.

People have finally started experimenting with different cards in the deck, but the shell never really changes. The deck that won the SCG open after Khans was released was only 1 card off from the list that won Legacy Champs weeks later. There has been a little innovation since then, but it has been focused at beating the U/R mirror with additional maindeck pyroblasts.

The burn spells besides Lightning Bolt are clearly up for consideration. The first list had 2 Forked Bolts and 1 Chain Lightning, which screams uncertainty. If the default plan is to kill creatures, Forked Bolt is the better card. If the main goal is to race, Chain Lightning is the better candidate. The first thing I would try is either 3 forked bolts, or shave another card for a full set of chain lightning.
I have truly hated playing with Daze in this deck. There are some free wins in casting turn 1 Delver and Dazing their first spell, but it goes downhill fast from there. The lack of Stifle and Wasteland mean Daze is only good for the first few turns of the game. Drawing Daze off of Treasure Cruise in the midgame is miserable, as statistically only 2 of the 3 cards will be nonlands. However, even with all the things I dislike about the card, it has its merits. Fast combo is one of the deck’s biggest weaknesses, and anything to supplement Force of Will is a welcome addition.
The seventeenth land might be unnecessary. Most Temur delver lists ran 18 lands, but 4 of them were Wastelands. Going down to 16 lands could be problematic against other Wasteland decks, but the most popular version of Delver (this U/R version) doesn’t play them.

I’ve spent the last couple weeks scouring tournament results. Those results, along with some personal experience with Delver decks have lead me to the following possible alternatives.

Fireblast – This card is probably limited to being a singleton, but it seems like great reach to close a game. Acting as a Black Lotus for Treasure Cruise just seems sweet.
Spell Pierce – Likely the next best counterspell after Daze and Force of Will for this deck. Leaving up mana is somewhat at odds with this deck, but fast combo decks are likely the worst matchup
Price of Progress – Very good at closing out games, but it lacks versatility. It is also somewhat at odds with the deck, as the deck’s mana is tolerable at best. Running off of the basic lands can put unnecessary strain on the order for casting spells.
Dig Through Time – This would just act as Treasure Cruise number 5, but that could easily be worth the additional mana. Treasure Cruise is the stronger card in this deck, since the power level of all the cards is close and the second blue mana is a real cost.
Cabal Therapy – This would require substantial changes to the manabase, but it could be worth the cost. Most of the fast combo decks use discard effects to circumvent counterspells, so proactively fighting combo is a strong plan. Cabal Therapy’s flashback happens to be free with Young Pyromancer, and Gitaxian Probe remvoes all the guesswork.
Sudden Shock – Uncounterable burn seems fantastic against anything other than dedicated combo. Stoneforge Mystic, Deathrite Shaman, and Monistary Swiftspear (specifically this one) are all strong targets. However, 2 mana for 2 damage is a terrible rate.
Gutshot – Free spell to pump swiftspear. However, it doesn’t kill much outside of the mirror, D&T, and elves. Forked bolt is almost definitely better.
Mutagenic Growth – Originally to dodge bolt, but it only protects swiftspear and flipped delvers. It also doesn’t stop Swords to Plowshares. It is a free spell though, so it’s worth considering
Preordain – Is eight the optimal number of blue cantrip spells? This is a lot harder to test, but it could easily be worth including. There is certainly a ceiling on this kind of effect, since there needs to be a critical mass of spells capable of winning a game.
Thought Scour – No selection offered, but this clears cards from Ponder and Brainstorm. Great with submerge or against enlightened tutor. Against people that want to cruise, it’s probably too dangerous.
Fire//Ice – This is a versatile effect that can pitch to Force of Will. However, it is probably not worth the additional cost over Forked Bolt. I also tend to attribute this card to missing the T8 of a GP, so I’m pretty biased against it.
Grim Lavamancer – An additional strong creature if 12 isn’t the correct number. However, it doesn’t solve the deck’s weakness to Golgari Charm, Zealous Persecution, Night of Soul’s Betrayal, and Electrickery. It also competes with Treasure Cruise for fuel, which is a mark against it.
True Name Nemesis – Mini Progenitus is a great blocker and a decent attacker, but probably not what this deck needs. It blocks Tarmogoyf and gets through some small chunks of damage, but it isn’t a great rate in a deck that curves out at 2 mana.
Sulfuric Vortex – This is a good sideboard card, but it doesn’t strengthen the deck versus most of the field. Combo is too fast to matter, and U/R Delver already has a good matchup against a lot of the midrange decks. It is very strong versus Miracles.
Echoing Truth – This can serve as a catch-all for anything problematic. Chalice of the Void, Tarmogoyf, Empty the Warrens Tokens, and countless other things. It might be better off as a Boomerang, as that can buy a turn against Maze of Ith. Either way, this is probably a sideboard card at best. The deck wants to be proactive when possible.
Ball Lightning – Because ball lightning. But not really. It’s sweet though.

After working with the deck for a while, this is the version I’m currently testing.
