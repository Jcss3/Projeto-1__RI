Shambling into Standard

By Byron King
@DoneTheMath

 

Black aggressive decks have held a special place in my heart for several years now. My favorite deck of all time featured such notable hits as Vampire Lacerator, Pulse Tracker, and . I’ve played more than my fair share of Gravecrawlers and Geralf’s Messangers, and I had a grand time doing it.

Needless to say, I was pumped when Dread Wanderer was spoiled. I preordered a set, completed my playsets of Diregraf Colossus and Relentless Dead, and… had life get in the way. I didn’t get to put anything together. The only Magic I got to play on release weekend was FNM, where I played a carbon copy of Gerry T’s U/W cycling deck. I didn’t even think that list was particularly good, but I didn’t have time to build a manabase to include Nahiri and Magma Spray. That deck was fun and all, but I was crushed when I saw Zombies being played to some success at the Open in Atlanta.

No more. I’ll be getting in my reps with Dread Wanderer from now until the PT. Probably much longer than that, but it’ll depend on what gets cooked up for that event. Anyway, let’s look at some of the lists from the past few days.

I’ll be focusing on dedicated Zombie decks. The mono black aggro deck that made the top 8 of SCG Atlanta isn’t what I’m looking for. Neither are the Prized Amalgam lists like this:

These are the lists I found that include a white splash. The only maindeck addition that white gives is Wayward Servant. It seems good, but I certainly wouldn’t splash for just it. The real prize is in the sideboard, where a full set of Gideon, Ally of Zendikar are lurking. At least that’s true for two of the three lists.  Gideon is a single card threat that is serviceable against control. More importantly, Gideon really hammers combo and ramp players. In an open metagame, that’s important. Anguished Unmaking is the other big bonus for playing white, though none of these lists really seem to leverage the universal answer.

That rounds out the lists I found online. Six lists, and there are some pretty big differences between them.

 

The white splash is the biggest difference. I went over some of the pros above, including Wayward Servant, Gideon, Ally of Zendikar, and Anguished Unmaking. The biggest downside is the mana. Aether Hub seems completely unplayable in a list so heavily committed to black mana symbols. Twelve white producing lands is already low for a double white card, and don’t get me started on the lack of sideboarded lands for the MTGO list. If nothing else, there should be some copies of Forsaken Sanctuary in these lists. At least two of them, probably a full set.

Apart from the mana, these lists are solid. Wayward Servant is a solid two drop that actually counts as a Zombie. It also doesn’t come with the strict mana requirements of Relentless Dead.

Shambling Vent is one of the few ways to get an edge from your mana base. A pile of Swamps certainly doesn’t offer any insurance against flooding out. It’s not a huge bonus, but every bit counts.

Gideon, Ally of Zendikar is a great plan for an unknown metagame. Synergies are great, but sometimes an opponent just needs to die before shenanigans happen. Smashing for five a turn is a good way to facilitate that.

Anguished Unmaking is the last big pull to white. A universal answer that deals with Drake Haven and Dynavolt Tower would already be appealing. Beyond that, it handles planeswalkers and recursive threats like Prized Amalgam and opposing Zombies. I would be playing at least two, possibly three copies until the metagame settles down.

 


Beyond Lord of the Accursed, Metallic Mimic is the other “lord” available to Zombies. The biggest strike against it is the required play pattern it necessitates. Metallic Mimic has to come down before any other Zombie to give a bonus. Lords are naturally going to draw removal spells from an opponent, as they provide the biggest boost to damage for an aggressive draw. Needing to play your lord before playing any other creature ensures an opponent will have removal to deal with it.

The other strike against Metallic Mimic comes down to how it pigeonholes you into the aggressive deck. Most other creatures in this deck all have ways to gain value or grind out the opponent. The deck has an aggressive slant, but Metallic Mimic doesn’t really offer the option to pivot.

 


The honorary Zombie, Scrapheap Scrounger has been making a name for itself in Mardu Vehicles for a while now. Honestly, Zombies would have a better time of it if Scrapheap Scrounger hadn’t already made it clear how important Magma Spray would be in the coming months. Oh well, this is where we are now.

I don’t think this is a great card in Zombies, but I’m pretty sure the mono black decks need to play it. Particularly considering my dislike of Metallic Mimic, there aren’t a bunch of other options. Relentless Dead fits in by default and white gets Wayward Servant. If you want to stick to the Zombie theme after that, you’re down to Miasmic Mummy. Scrapheap Scrounger is miles better than Miasmic Mummy, Zombie synergy or not.

 


Before I get into Liliana’s Mastery, I want to discuss an old deck.

This deck is years old, and I remember hating it when it came out. The curve of one drop all the way up to five in an aggressive deck always felt like a pipe dream. The deck needed enough lands to support those expensive cards, so the beatdown draws always felt underwhelming.

I’ve grown as a Magic player since then. The biggest lesson I learned from this deck was to utilize the strongest cards available. Gravecrawler and Diregraf Ghoul weren’t the best cards in this deck. Thundermaw Hellkite, Falkenrath Aristocrat, and Hellrider were the powerhouse cards, and everything else was more of a supporting cast.

Liliana’s Mastery doesn’t feel like the strongest card in a Zombie list. It’s a fine card, but I’m not sure it’s worth the deck slots. Not just for the copies of Mastery, but the additional pair of lands the deck really needs to support a five cost card.

While Liliana’s Mastery requires additional lands, the deck simply doesn’t have a useful four drop to play. Gideon might be worth it for white decks, but black simply doesn’t have a useful four drop to play. Beyond that, Zombies don’t have a great outlet for mana flood. Cryptbreaker can bin excess lands for Zombie tokens, but that isn’t using those lands to cast Liliana’s Mastery. Relentless Dead is the only other big mana sink in the deck, but it doesn’t need much help. If you expect to trigger Relentless Dead, you’re going to have to devote a whole turn to getting another creature back.

 


This appears to be the mana sink option for decks going down the Liliana’s Mastery path. If I really wanted to pursue this plan, I feel like the white lands are the better plan. You don’t gain actual value from the lands (besides Shambling Vent), but you gain access to a better suite of sideboard options. I don’t know if this really adds much to the mono black deck, but I imagine it’s better than Swamp 22+. I think I’d stay away in decks that don’t go up the chain to Liliana’s Mastery.

 


I’m not a huge fan of this card in sideboards. Lay Bare the Heart seems better under far more circumstances.

 


This sideboard card is included for control matchups. Missing Magma Spray, Horribly Awry, and Pull from Tomorrow are all pretty unforgivable. Missing Archangel Avacyn is the biggest downside, but Lay Bare the Heart will at least let you know you need to play around the Angel.  Discarding instead of exiling is unfortunate, but it still seems worth the cost.

 


I was almost done before I realized that Binding Mummy wasn’t the same as Sparring Mummy. This card made an appearance in a few of the sideboards, and I originally ignored it. Upon actually reading the card, Binding Mummy seems like a good option for to race aggressive decks and completely horrible against control. The combination of a tap effect and various instances of Menace on several good Zombies should make blocking a nightmare. I might play a copy or two in the maindeck, but I’m less excited about it in the sideboard. Every deck is going to load up on more removal against an aggressive opponent, and Binding Mummy doesn’t offer any bonuses without staying in play for a turn or two. It has better odds of surviving in game one.

Now that I’ve looked at some of the cards I consider more controversial, it’s time to work on some decks of my own.

This seems like where I want to start with a mono black version. Liliana doesn’t seem likely to be at its best in a deck like this, but I don’t have much else to try without reaching up to Liliana’s Mastery. I’d rather start with a low curve and see how that treats me.

Looking at this list more, I’m starting to see the appeal of Liliana’s Mastery and Metallic Mimic. This list has a few more duds than I really want to play.  Pushing the anthem effects to the limits might be one of the more powerful options for Zombies.

Mono black’s sideboard is the weakest link in the chain. There simply isn’t much room for customization. Never // Return is the best answer for planeswalkers. Transgress the Mind and Lay Bare the Heart  are for control decks. Dispossess deals with Aetherworks Marvel, along with some possible applications against Torrential Gearhulk. Gifted Aetherborn is a fine answer for currently nonexistant aggro decks. It is also good against the giant monsters in G/B, even if it is horribly weak to opposing Fatal Push. Lastly, you can use some slots to customize your removal spells, but that usually feels like wasted sideboad slots to me.

Whether or not that’s true, I’m more interested in working on a W/B Zombie deck.

This looks like where I’d want to start with a B/W list. I added the Forsaken Sanctuary over Aether Hub and went up to 21 lands. Wayward Servant immediately assumes the position of dominant two drop. I want to start with Scrapheap Scrounger, so I left out Binding Mummy.  If control doesn’t make much of an appearance over the coming weeks, I can see that decision being horribly wrong.

White gives the deck some actual choices for sideboarding. Anguished Unmaking trades the zombie token from Never // Return for the ability to answer anything, and I’ve sung Gideon’s praises several times already. These feel like answers worth the cost of a splash.

That’s the compilation of my Zombie work to date. Best of luck with your endeavors!
