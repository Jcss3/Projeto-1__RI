November 28, 2016

2016 Cube Updates

By Byron King
@DoneTheMath

 

With the Christmas season approaching, my cube needs to be dusted off and updated.  During most of the year, firing a cube draft can be a bit of a hassle.  The holidays usually bring people back into the area, and the occasional vacation days from work sometimes match up with enough other people.

I’ve had various Cubes together for years now.  My current Cube is sitting in a box where I haven’t touched it for about six months.  At this point, it needs some updates.  There are multiple sets that I have reviewed, but I haven’t actually tracked down any cards and added them to my cube yet.

I’m going to start with Oath of the Gatewatch and cover the whole year up to this point.

 

Oath of the Gatewatch


In generic cubes, black is usually considered the worst color.  The aggressive black cards are worse than white or red, the controlling cards are awful compared to blue, and the best black cards require excessive amounts of black mana.  Kalitas, Traitor of Ghet is a great addition to any cube.

_

This is one of the times where I get the benefit of writing in the future.  Chandra, Flamecaller certainly has a place in cube, but it is more tenuous with the printing of Chandra, Torch of Defiance.  Chandra, Flamecaller is a great card in midrange to controlling red decks, while the newest Chandra can fit into all but the most aggressive red decks.  I try to keep the number of planeswalkers in my cube down to a reasonable level, but Chandra, Flamecaller is probably the third best red planeswalker after Koth of the Hammer and Chandra, Torch of Defiance.  If your cube doesn’t support big mana red decks (Wildfire), Chandra, Pyromanster or Sarkhan, the Dragonspeaker might be better simply because of the reduced mana cost.

 


A problem I notice in a lot of cubes is the overwhelming number of red cards that focus on early aggression.  Goblin Dark-Dwellers is a good addition, particularly if you need more midrange red cards.  It also scales well, with powered cubes offering better targets to recur.
I’m also looking to cast some free Ancestral Visions, since I don’t think I’ll be doing that in Modern anytime soon.

 


Goblin Bushwacker was always a little unappealing between the color restrictions and the smaller body.  Goblin Bushwacker should be a good addition to red aggressive sections, and the easier mana restrictions allow for a splash.

 


This Nissa is good, but green is overflowing with good options for planeswalkers already.  I get the benefit of knowing about the newest Nissa, but she is another five cost planeswaker that will compete with Garruk, Primal Hunter and Nissa, Worldwaker.  Larger cubes, or cubes that put an emphasis on tokens should use Nissa, Voice of Zendikar.  My cube is fairly small, but I’m still going to consider her.

_

Oath of Nissa just feels like a placeholder card to me.  There are so many good options for green in cube that I would just want to add something more interesting.  There is virtually no deck that is ever going to be excited about Oath of Nissa, and blue already adds a ton of card draw and filtering to the cube.  I will probably stay away from Oath of Nissa in my cube.

 


Sylvan Advocate is a great option for cubes with an aggressive green section.  Smaller cubes, including mine, often skip the aggressive cards in green.  I won’t be adding Sylvan Advocate to my cube.

 


Azorius really blows most of the other guilds out of the water with card quality.  Sphinx’s Revelation, Detention Sphere, Supreme Verdict, Dragonlord Ojutai, Narset Transcendent, Geist of Saint Traft, Reflector Mage, Spell Queller, and Dovin Baan are cards worthy of a spot.  Most cubes run between one and five multicolor cards, so some chopping will be necessary for my cube.

 


The cycle of creature lands and fast lands have been completed over the last year.  Fetches, duals, and shock lands have always been a staple in generic cubes, but there is usually a mess of lands people pick from after that.  The reasonable options now are
Fetch, shock, and dual lands
Creature lands
Fast lands (Blooming Marsh cycle)
Filter lands (necessary if <> Eldrazi are supported)
Pain lands (necessary if <> Eldrazi are supported)
Miscellaneous utility lands
I would expect most cubes to have 50-70 lands in total, with 10-20 being utility lands (Academy Ruins, Strip Mine, etc…)
I expect I will run the creature lands in addition to the staples, then probably move towards five color mana fixers and utility lands.  My cube isn’t big enough to use additional lands slots

 

Shadows over Innistrad


There are already plenty of good white cards that cost five mana, but Archangel Avacyn is among the best.  I might finally have to kick out Karmic Guide

 


There are a few tiers of white removal spells.  Swords to Plowshares, Path to Exile, Oblivion Ring, and Council’s Judgement tend to sit around the top.  There is a lower level with cards like Condemn and Journey to Nowhere.  Declaration in Stone is going to fall in that category, and I think it is worse than Journey to Nowhere.  I probably won’t add it to my cube.

 


Adding a theme to cubes always felt a little weird, unless the cube is designed to focus on a bunch of them.  White incidentially has a lot of aggressive humans, so it might be worth considering a real push for the theme.  The biggest benefit is giving white a separate identity from red.  The problem is that around three and four mana, white’s aggressive cards shift to random creature types.  I don’t think Champion of the Parish, Thalia’s Lieutenant, and Paliano Vanguard significantly improve the color identity, but I might still try it.

 


This card is great.  Standard over the last year has shown that Thraben Inspector belongs in cube.  It is also a human, in case I do pursue that plan.

_

I don’t think I’m going to be adding Town Gossipmonger, but it is another human for that theme.

 


Most of the times I have seen Thing in the Ice in cube, it didn’t really work.  It usually won’t win the game by itself, but it requires a spell heavy deck to even trigger it.  Those decks will usually need more reliable win conditions than a vanilla 7/8, even if it casts Evacuation.  One of the problems I have when working on a cube is my desire to add sweet cards that I rarely got to play in standard.  I probably need to stay away.

 


This is a good addition to a black aggressive section in cube.  I think I will be keeping that section in mine, so Asylum Visitor will make the cut.

 


One of the other options for black is a Zombie theme.  I’m a more hesitant about this one that the Human one, due to the mana requirements.  Zombies often have lots of black mana symbols in their cost, making splashes and multiple color decks difficult.  The payoff cards for Zombies are also worse than the Human options.  I just don’t think this is the way I want to go.

 


Pretty much a repeat of Diregraff Colossus.  I don’t think I’ll be using it, but it is worth a consideration.

 


Another card that works with a black aggressive section.  It offers a discard outlet for reanimator strategies, but there are better options for simple discard outlets.  The crossover potential for themes is a good bonus.

 


This is essentially a vanilla 2/1 for one mana with no drawbacks in red.  That should kick out something like Jackel Pup in most cubes.

_

Evaluation of Duskwatch Recruiter will vary depending on how much of an aggressive green section a cube has.  Mine doesn’t have one, so I will be considering it for the card advantage and acceleration benefits.  Larger cubes should definitely add it.

 


Green likes giant monsters and putting lands into play.  Green decks often need some card advantage.  This seems like an easy auto include.

 


Nahiri, the Harbinger will fight Ajani Vengeant for space in most cubes.  Nahiri is probably the more powerful card, but it is the less appealing option for aggressive decks.  Red and white usually have an aggressive section, so splashing will be common.  Those decks don’t really want card filtering or expensive, situational removal.  Ajani Vengeant is probably the better option for a well-rounded planeswalker.
That said, I like to push red away from aggro when possible, so I might include Nahiri instead.  I also have a huge desire to cheat giant monsters into play.

 


Vindicate is a better card, and I don’t really think that multiple copies of that effect are necessary.  Most cubes should not use Anguished Unmaking.

 


There are a ton of other Orzhov planeswalkers vying for cube spots.  The six mana cost makes this an inclusion in control and midrange decks, but not aggressive decks.  I’ll probably go with a cheaper mana cost alternative.

 


A good utility land to support a token theme.  Without tokens, Westvale Abbey really only fits in a green deck with a lot of mana ramp creatures.  Or an incredibly slow, grindy deck.  Seems a little narrow, but not completely unreasonable.

 

Eldritch Moon


Bruna, the Fading Light only makes the list because (spoiler) Gisela, the Broken Blade is a contender.  Melding is sweet, and who doesn’t play cube for the sweet stories?
I haven’t ended up on a deck with this Meld pair yet, so that is the biggest draw for me.  I’ll probably give Bruna a spot for a little while, just to see how it goes.

_

Gisela, on the other hand, is a serious consideration on her own.  I really like where she falls.  It is a great card against aggressive decks, but it isn’t game over since she only has three toughness.  She is still respectable against slower decks, so she isn’t just a sideboard card.  Plus, there is that sweet Meld potential, and that has to be worth something.  Right?

 


Aggressive flier with a great ability.  Seems like an easy inclusion to any cube that supports aggressive white decks.

 


I like that Thalia, Heretic Cathar falls right at the level of mildly annoying.  She is a slight slap in the face for people looking to do durdly things in cube, not the full on punch in the genitals that Armageddon can be.  She is also a good card in aggressive mirrors, since blocking won’t be an option anymore.  All but the smallest cubes should find room for Thalia, Heretic Cathar.

_

Elder Deep-Fiend is a great card.  It needs some support to go with it, since Emerge is a real deckbuilding cost.  I find that there are enough other things that blue is doing that it doesn’t really need to make room for Elder Deep-Fiend.  I expect some of the larger cubes should open a slot for it.  I won’t be adding it to my cube.

 


Pretty sure this is just worse than a bunch of other options.  Merfolk Looter, Looter il-Kor, and Jace, Vryn’s Prodigy all do this job better.  I did pick up a foil copy, just in case I change my mind.

_

Unrestricted removal in blue is a rare commodity.  If you like giving each color a range of options, this is one of the best for blue removal.  Personally, I like colors needing to stretch a little to find a good mix of threats and answers.  Larger cubes should probably play it, but I don’t think my cube will qualify for that.

 


Liliana, the Last Hope is a nightmare for most of the aggressive decks in cube.  Against control, she is pretty close to “Suspend 4: win the game”.  She is pretty awful against combo, but that is where Liliana of the Veil shines the brightest.  Black was lacking somewhat for a spread of good planeswalkers, so Liliana, the Last Hope should earn a spot.

 


Cryptbreaker is a fine card, but you really need to be abusing the graveyard in some way to make it shine.  It also happens to be a Zombie, but I don’t think I’m specifically going down that path.  It does make a playable card for both aggressive and midrange decks, but I doubt it is good enough to make the cut in most cubes.

 


Unlike Elder Deep-Fiend, Distended Mindbender should have a fairly easy home.  Black is much more likely to have creatures to Emerge with, with cards like Nekrataal or simply upgrading aggressive creatures.  Black is happy to have more good options, and I expect Distended Mindbender to find a home in most cubes.

 


Hanweir Garrison is a consideration for multiple reasons.  It is a respectable card.  Not quite Goblin Rabblemaster, but that is a fairly high bar to reach.  It has a fairly unrestricted mana cost, so multicolor aggressive decks can run it.  Lastly, it also has that sweet Meld potential.  I expect Hanweir Battlements to make the cut in midrange and large cubes, so Hanweir Garrison should at least be considered on that regard.

 


This is a slam dunk addition to cube.  I know there are a lot of Azorius cards that deserve consideration, but Spell Queller is a messed up Magic card.  As long as a cube has any multicolor cards, Spell Queller should be the first Azorius card.

_

Emrakul, the Promised End does some great stuff when you cast it.  Playing your opponent’s next turn is great.  Unfortunately, this Emrakul is just a big dumb idiot unless you actually cast it.  Reanimating it or otherwise cheating it into play is not ideal.  That makes it less appealing to a wide range of strategies.  On the other hand, Annihilate is a miserable mechanic to play against.  It is brutal enough that some people will want to switch out Emrakul, the Aeons Torn for a reasonable replacement.  I like the shenanigans that Emrakul, the Aeons Torn can cause, so I won’t be making the switch in my cube.

 


Red doesn’t have quite the same level of utility lands as the other colors.  White has Karakas, Blue has Shelldock Isle and sometimes Tolarian Academy, black has Urborg, Volrath’s Stronghold, and Phyrexian Tower, and green has Gaea’s Cradle and Treetop Village.  Red gets Ghitu Encampment?  Red often gets the biggest advantage out of Wasteland/Strip Mine/Rashadan Port, but Hanweir Battlements can give the color a specific value land.  I expect it to be a common adoption.

 

Conspiracy, Take the Crown


This is another consideration for the Human subtheme in white.  It won’t be making the cut for any other reason.

 


I’m pretty confident that Arcane Savant is completely, absurdly busted in a generic cube.  The dream is a turn five Cruel Ultimatum, but just gaining access to off-color cards is a huge advantage.  I will try it in my cube, but I don’t expect to take it out pretty quick after people get sick of it.

 


I like the information that this provides, and a 1/3 flying blocker isn’t the most embarrassing card to have in your sideboard.  This is a fun kind of “draft matters” card.

 


This is going to range from awful to awesome depending on the colors that get named.  Cards that require documentation seem like a pain for cube, so I think there will be some resistance to Regicide due to the hassle.  It might still be worth trying for a while simply because it is such a unique type of card.

 


Granzo, Havoc Raiser seems great in a cube that supports tokens.  Even without that support, he might be good enough in a generic aggressive deck.  The mana cost will be the limiting factor, as there are plenty of red two drops that cost double red.

 


I love the randomness.  I also love cheating giant idiots into play way too early.  I’m going to be baised with Volatile Chimera because I really want to play with it.  Realiztically, it is going to be incredible or awful with little chance of being anywhere between those extremes.  It will fit best in some kind of ramp, reanimator, or sneak attack deck that didn’t find a better use for giant creatures.  I’ll be trying it in my cube, but I can see it train wrecking a few other strategies a little too often.

 


Animus of Predation requires a search of each cube for the number of creatures with relevant keywords.  If you can’t see at least ~4 creaures in a given draft with a keyword, you probably don’t have enough keywords to run Animus of Predation.

_

This is a great addition to Cogwork Librarian.  The effect is fun for any cube that uses the “draft matters” cards.

 


Daretti, Ingenious Iconoclast has some stiff competiton in the Rakdos section of cube.  Sarkan the Mad isn’t strong competition for a planeswalker, but Rakdos Cackler, Terminate, Dreadbore, Blightning, Kolaghan’s Command, Olivia Voldaren, Falkenrath Aristocrat, and Blightning are all vying for a small number of spots.  Daretti, Ingenious Iconoclast is worth a slot if you support tokens, artifacts, or stress midrange decks over combo decks.

 


Sorin already dominates the Orzhov color pair for planeswalkers.  I absolutely love the design on Kaya, Ghost Assassin, but I don’t think it is as good as either of the four mana Sorin planeswalkers.

 


If you add Conspiracies to cube, Emissary’s Ploy seems like a fun addition.  Adding Mother of Runes and Isamaru to a red deck seems good, or a Dimir deck might want to run a Birds of Paradise.  However, they tend to really warp cube sessions, since you get the bonus for about three matches instead of a single game.

 


This card is extremely unfair.  All but the most degerate cubes should stay away from this Conspiracy.

 

Kaladesh


Cubes, particularly ones with power, often want some Disenchant effects.  Fragmentize is a nice, cheap answer.

_

Fumigate seems better than Hallowed Burial or Rout for a five cost Wrath effect.  Not sure if you want that effect, but Fumigate will be there if you do.

 


Cube is filled with lots of great instants.  Torrential Gearhulk will find a home in cube.

 


If black moves away from an aggressive slant, Gonti, Lord of Luxury seems great.  I want to find room for it in my cube.

 


Black has some decent top end cards, between Grave Titan, the Delve cards, and some Reanimation targets.  Noxious Gearhulk is a sweet card, but it might be a little difficult to squeeze it in. If you promote midrange, grindy cube decks, Noxious Gearhulk is a good addition.  If you push more for combo decks, Noxious Gearhulk should stay on the outside looking in.

 


A great aggressive, recurring threat.  That’s exactly what a black aggressive card should look like.

 


Chandra has a use in aggressive, midrange, and control decks, along with ramp for any deck that is interested.  She hasn’t had the best run in standard so far, but I’m sure she is good enough to join the cube team.

_

I think I prefer Nissa, Worldwaker and Garruk, Primal Hunter at five mana.  Green just has so many good options to pick between.

 


This just seems like an easy upgrade for Wolfir Silverheart.  Tons of power for a low cost, and it works particularly well with Birds of Paradise and friends.

 


Dovan Baan just seems like the stereotypical Azorius planeswalker.  Venser and Narset both do some cool things, but I think that neither of them fit particularly in the normally controlling Azorius decks.  I’m still hoping to see a Plow Under cast off of Narset, so I might leave Narset in my cube.  Dovan Baan is probably the better addition.

_

If you are stretching for cards like Pristine Talisman for mana accelerators, Cultivator’s Caravan should easily make the cut.

 


This is a good addition for any aggressive deck while appearing subpar for most midrange and all control decks.  This is a good inclusion if aggressive selections are looking a little weak, or if you want to bolster your artifact section.

 


Cubes have a surprisingly small number of 3/x creatures compared with 2/x creatures.  That can make crewing Skysovereign, Consul Flagship more difficult that it would otherwise appear.  Large cubes, particlularly ones looking for planeswalker removal, should consider Skysovereign, Consul Flagship.  My cube is probably a little too small for the giant vehicle.

_

This is right next to Spell Queller as clear inclusions into cube.  The power level is sky high, and every aggressive and midrange deck can run it.  The current standard format is a good example of why cube should include Smuggler’s Copter.

_

Like the creature lands in Battle for Zendikar and Oath of the Gatewatch, the completed cycle of fast lands makes it worth considering adding the full cycle to cube.  I like this cycle a lot, but I think  creature lands are a better choice.  If your aggressive decks need more help, these fast lands are a bigger benefit to them than to the slower decks.

 

Commander 2016


Even with a storm section, and I still don’t think Magus of the Will is a good addition to cube.  The turn delay and the vulnerability to removal just look too weak to overcome.

 


This is a supercharged Pentad Prism that comes with the strengths and weaknesses of a creature.  I will probably try it, but I need to see it in action a few times to really get a good feel for it.

 


There are a lot of ways for this to be worse than Gilded Lotus.  Coming into play tapped is a huge downgrade, and there are plenty of decks that can’t get this up to three different colors of mana.  I don’t think Prismatic Geoscape is a good addition to any cube.

 


A good addition to the Evolving Wilds and Teramorphic Expanse team.  I like including as much fixing as possible for all five colors, and Ash Barrens will be a welcome addition.

 

That covers the cards I wanted to consider for cube in 2016.  There have been plenty of new options, and some clearly powerful cards to add to the team.  I’m looking forward to trying out my new cube.  Hopefully yours is up and running too.
