Conspiracy, Take the Crown

By Byron King
@DoneTheMath

 

I’ve only got one point I want to cover before diving into the Conspiracy, take the Crown review.  I rate almost all of the Monarch cards highly for Commander, simply because they are a novel effect.  More than that, any one of those cards introduces the Monarch title for the rest of the game.  The extra card is enough incentive to encourage attacking and drive a game forward.  It is exciting, new, and players will want to try it out.  The cards will start out popular, and I don’t actually expect that excitement to wane for a long time.

With that out of the way, let’s get started with the review!

 

She will generate a little bit of Commander hype simply from being a Legendary creature.  Melee is a sweet ability for aggressive Commander decks, but those are traditionally underwhelming.  There is also a lot of competition from other Boros commanders, and Adriana, Captain of the Guard just feels like more of the same.  No other formats will be interested in Adriana.

Arcane Savent only does something in a draft, and a vanilla 3/3 for five mana isn’t playable anywhere else.  This trigger seems awesome in Cube, where there are plenty of great options to pick from.  Arcane Savant can also salvage otherwise wasted draft picks after you have moved out of a color.  Any Cube that includes “draft matters” Conspiracy cards will want Arcane Savant.

I may be slightly biased about Arcane Savant in Cube, since I just see a Cruel Ultimatum for five mana.  Realistically, almost any spell in Cube will make Arcane Savant an outstanding card.

As long as a draft isn’t involved, Archdemon of Paliano doesn’t have a drawback.  There is a chance that some Commander player is interested, particularly since Demon is a useful creature type.

Some of the voting cards from the original Conspiracy set saw play, but the default choice in a two player game needed to be good.  Council’s Judgement and Coercive Portal are good examples of a successful voting card.  Capital Punishment is a good example of a voting card that will never see play outside of multiplayer.  It cares about the number of votes, so the effect is greatly diminished in a two player game.

Commander decks might have some interest in Capital Punishment.  Four players means that the card has an effect that could warrant the mana cost.  It seems difficult enough to engineer the correct vote that I wouldn’t play it.  People will vote in their own best interest, so when you need a Wrath effect, your opponents will be discarding cards.  When you need the discard, opponents will be sacrificing creatures that they might not even have.

Our first Monarch card.  In case you haven’t seen the token, the Monarch draws a card during their end step and combat damage to the Monarch steals the title.  Becoming the Monarch is a serious benefit, and it is worth considering the Monarch cards in Constructed.  For a card to make it, it will need to cost less than four or come with some additional effect.  Otherwise, Coercive Portal is just better, and it isn’t seeing any play outside of Vintage Workshop decks.

Unfortunately, Custodi Lich probably won’t be seeing any Legacy play.  The body is rather weak, and the extra bonus doesn’t justify the cost.  All of that doesn’t matter in Commander, where Custodi Lich should prove to be very popular.

Normally, I go over Planeswalker abilities one at a time and then discuss the whole card.  I’m going to skip that this time.  Daretti is a cool card that just doesn’t have a home.  This is no Dack Faden in Legacy or Vintage.  A 1/1 Defender is not the same as two looting effects.  A Planeswalker’s ability to defend itself matters, but only if the other abilities do something.  Sacrificing an artifact to kill a creature is not a good deal, since you end up neutral on card advantage.  Synergistic artifact decks exist in Legacy, but they usually rely on Tezzeret, Agent of Bolas.  Daretti, Ingenious Iconoclast might find a home with him.  Vintage artifact decks rely on Mishra’s Workshop and definitely don’t want a multicolor card.

Having reread the card, I realized that the minus ability can destroy artifacts too.  That gives it more of a shot in Vintage, since everyone has some artifacts.  In particular, it can blow up opposing Null Rods that might be causing problems.  Daretti still feels like a long shot for Vintage, since it requires two colors of mana and neither of them are blue.  I’m pretty confidant that Daretti doesn’t have a home currently, and he isn’t strong enough to warrant a new deck.

Commander decks would probably be interested if the colors weren’t so weird.  Most artifact decks in Commander are Esper, Izzet, or just red, and Daretti can’t slot into any of those decks.  Maybe the four color Commander decks will give him a home, but I don’t know where he will go right now.

Cubes might be interested in Daretti if they already have a heavy artifact theme, but I doubt it.  Rakdos has an abundance of options for Cube, so it will be hard for Daretti, Ingenious Iconoclast to make the cut.

Recruiter of the Guard is one of the clear plants for competitive Magic.  The comparison to Imperial Recruiter is obvious, so the first places to look for homes are in Imperial Painter and Aluren.  Aluren is a reasonable home for Recruiter of the Guard, simply adding white instead of red.  Imperial Painter isn’t a fit for Recruiter of the Guard, since Painter’s Servant has three toughness.

This is the other big plant for competitive Magic.  Sanctum Prelate is a strange callback to Chalice of the Void, but without the need for ramp.  Sanctum Prelate is almost impossible to get into play on turn one, so it won’t completely shut down decks the way Chalice of the Void can, and it has no effect on creatures at all.  On the other hand, Sanctum Prelate can nullify higher converted mana cost cards without needing to ramp.  Worried about Natural Order, Jace, the Mind Sculptor, Terminus, or Ad Nauseum?  Sanctum Prelate has your back.

Eternal formats will jump on this card, specifically Death and Taxes in Legacy.  Shutting off all the spot removal and deck manipulation spells will be worth invalidating any future Swords to Plowshares and Aether Vials.  I don’t know if Recruiter of the Guard will make a big splash in Death and Taxes, but I am confident that Sanctum Prelate will make the cut.

Prison Commander decks might run Sanctum Prelate, but identifying the correct number will be difficult.  With such a wide range of spells available in Commander, you are really just taking shot in the dark. There are similar concerns for Cube.  The range of mana costs to eliminate is smaller, but Sanctum Prelate runs a bigger chance of nullifying your own cards.  I doubt it will be popular in either format.
