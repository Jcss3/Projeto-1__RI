EMA Rares and Mythics at a Glance

By Byron King
@DoneTheMath

 

EMA has finally arrived.  Maybe you’ll get a chance to open a few packs.  Maybe squeeze in a few drafts.  Either way, you can end up with some Rares that you might not really want.  Below, I’ve indicated how likely the cards are to appeal to different subsections of the Magic community.  If you are looking to find a target trading audience, or if you are looking for a justification to keep your new card, this should start you on the right path.

I have each format marked as High, Medium, Low, and None.  They are rough estimates, unless a card is already banned in that format.  I was a little more harsh in the Competitive category, since these cards have already had a chance to break out into Legacy or Vintage.  I didn’t mark None for any of the Commander cards unless they were banned, since someone can always find a use for a card in Commander.  For cube, I am referring to a more general layout with generic good cards.  Some cubes have themes or restrictions that change how useful a card would be, but that is a little too detailed to go into for every rare.

 

 
 

Starting off the list with a complete dud.  Nothing to see here, just open a better pack next time.

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: None
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

The Legacy Enchantress deck uses Argothian Enchantress as a key draw engine.  Unfortunately, Serra’s Sanctum and Savannah are on the reserved list.  Considering some of the alternatives, this isn’t the worst option for a budget Legacy deck.  Just consider that the cards needed for Enchantress don’t really carry over to any other deck.

Constructed Appeal: Medium – Cornerstone of a lesser known Legacy deck
Cube Appeal: Low
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

This card is busted.  People figured that out a long time ago and banned it in pretty much everything.  It is restricted in Vintage, so it can see a little play there.  Other than that, it sees a decent amount of play in Cube.

Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: None
 
 
 

We have seen Baleful Strix in a Planechase deck and a preconstructed Commander deck.  It isn’t the kind of flashy card that begs for a place in Commander decks.  Some decks will run it, but likely won’t go out of their way to find one.  Legacy Sultai decks have made good use of Baleful Strix, since it is essentially a removal spell that draws a card.  That’s a great value for two mana.

Constructed Appeal: Medium – Roleplayer in a popular Legacy deck
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

A card originally introduced in Conspiracy, Brago hasn’t made an appearance in Legacy or Vintage.  He does see a moderate amount of Cube play.  His Legendary creature type makes him a good candidate for Commander, along with his powerful ability.

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: Medium
Commander Appeal: High
 
 
 

Braids, Cabal Minion provides a strong ability, but Wizards hasn’t printed much to pair with it in recent years.  It’s a little too expensive for Legacy or Vintage, and it’s banned in Commander.  Some Cubes are built with a Braids/Smokestack theme, but that is about the extent of play that Braids, Cabal Minion can enjoy.

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: Medium
Commander Appeal: None
 
 
 

This one is a plant for the Eternal Masters limited format.  Fringe Commander play is the limit for Call the Skybreaker

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: None
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

Maybe not the most exciting reprint, but it is nice to have more in circulation.  Chrome Mox has only been printed in Mirrodin and as an old GP promo, so there aren’t that many around.  It makes an appearance in almost every Legacy deck that is trying to actually win the game on the first turn or two.  Unfortunately, most Chrome Mox decks need either Underground Sea or Lion’s Eye Diamond.  Or both.  It is a popular Cube card, but not the kind of mana acceleration Commander players look for.

Constructed Appeal: High
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 
 

That is some sweet new art.  Unfortunately, that’s the only real consolation for opening this in a $10 booster pack.  Cube players will probably be interested in switching out for better art, but Control Magic wasn’t an expensive card to get before EMA.  Commander players are probably more interested in cards like Blatant Thievery.

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

Umm… what?

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: None
Commander Appeal: Low (realistically none)
 
 
 

This Conspiracy card has made huge waves in Vintage.  There is a plethora of valuable artifacts to swindle in Vintage, and the looting ability is always appreciated.  It is also blue, so free bonus points for pitching to Force of Will.  Dack Fayden also makes an occasional appearance in Midrange/Control decks in Legacy.  Usually in some kind of Grixis deck.

Constructed Appeal: High – Vintage powerhouse and fringe Legacy player
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: High
 
 
 

Deathrite Shaman is banned in Modern, and for good reason.  Fetch lands take Deathrite from a bad Grim Lavamancer to a phenomenal Birds of Paradise.  Legacy is full of fetch lands, so Deathrite Shaman will always have a place there.  Shaman is much more inconsistent in Cube and Commander, so it will see much less play there.

Constructed Appeal: High
Cube Appeal: Medium
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

If you really need a Wheel of Fortune in Legacy, this is your best option.  Diminishing Returns occasionally shows up in sideboards as a target for Burning Wish.  Some Cubes that encourage combos might include Diminishing Returns, and any Nekusar, the Mindrazer Commander deck is going to include it.

Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: Low
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

The supposed second coming of Snapcaster Mage, but that never happened.  Dualcaster Mage can see some fringe Cube play and probably causes some great stories in Cube.

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: Low
Commander Appeal: Medium
 
 
 

Duplicant is a little too expensive for Legacy, and only enjoys fringe play in Mishra’s Workshop decks in Vintage.  Cubes often use Duplicant in their colorless section, as it is a flexible answer that can become a powerful threat.  Kind of like a worse, colorless Mind Control.  You can also Tinker for it.  Commander can do tons of things with Duplicant.

Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: High
 
 
 

This was a more popular Cube card when there were less good options for 1W.  It still makes some cubes, since the effect is very strong.  It just doesn’t fit into anything that isn’t a heavy white deck.  It is a Legendary creature, so it can see play as a Commander General.  I don’t think I’ve ever seen an Eight-and-a-Half-Tails Commander deck, and I can’t imagine it is popular.  Legacy and Vintage aren’t going to touch Eight-and-a-Half-Tails.

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: Medium
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

Enlightened Tutor makes an occasional appearance in several Legacy decks.  The most common use of Enlightened Tutor is to artificially increase the size of your sideboard.  One spot for Enlightened tutor becomes your second copy of Rest in Peace, Rule of Law, and Stony Silence.  Commander decks are often looking for additional tutors, so Enlightened Tutor will be a welcomed addition.  Cubes will occasionally use it.

Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: Low
Commander Appeal: High
 
 
 

The Reanimator deck exists because Entomb is legal in Legacy.  It functions as a tutor for your creature and a discard outlet all in a single card for a single mana.  Cube decks often incorporate a Reanimator theme, so Entomb will see some use there.  Commander decks can find plenty of uses too.

Constructed Appeal: High
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: Medium
 
 
 

I’m not going into detail on this one.  All I’ll say is that Force of Will is not a great card.  It is a necessary evil in formats where you could just die on turn one.  That still makes it necessary.  Everyone will be happy to use it.

Constructed Appeal: High
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: High
 
 
 

Future Sight is a weird way to draw a boatload of cards.  It still shows up in Cube, but Constructed decks won’t be interested. Commander decks might play it, but there are a lot of other options to pick from.

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: Medium
Commander Appeal: Medium
 
 
 

The only real home for Gamble in Constructed is Legacy Lands.  That deck doesn’t actually care if the card goes to the graveyard, since it is usually looking for Life from the Loam.  The Lands deck need a bunch of Restricted List cards, so Gamble wasn’t really the bar for people building the deck.

Gamble is also great if you are just dead on the inside and looking to feel some emotion when you play Magic.  Sometimes you just gotta feel something.  Don’t take that as a great strategic endorsement, because that is the opposite of what I am saying.

Constructed Appeal: Medium
Cube Appeal: Medium
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

Story Time.  I was at GP Columbus in 2012 playing Modern.  Next to me was Bronson Magnan (Winner of GP Lincoln/Hoth) with his trusty Aggro Loam deck.  His opponent was a local player with a home brew deck.  I got to watch Bronson fail to make Day 2 of the event with a hand full of Flame Jabs as Giant Solifuge turned sideways five times.

I probably would have found the whole experience more entertaining if I hadn’t been playing Tron against a red-green land destruction deck.  At best, I cast three spells in the entire match.  At least we made it to the Mexican place before happy hour ended, where we drown our sorrows in $5 32oz Margaritas.

Oh, you wanted to know about the strategic implications of Giant Solifuge?  There aren’t any.

Constructed Appeal: None (well, maybe one…)
Cube Appeal: None
Commander Appeal: None (It isn’t even a spider)
 
 

 

There is probably an EDH deck that wants this and can’t play Opposition.  Legacy, Vintage, and Cube can play Opposition if they even want it.

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: None
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

This is the namesake and one of the main win conditions of the Charbelcher deck in Legacy.  I think it is the only win condition in the Vintage version of the deck.  Goblin Charbelcher is pretty much the Force of Will check.  If they don’t have it, you should be set to win.  Fair decks aren’t going to touch this card.

Unfortunately, any deck running Goblin Charbelcher is going to run Lion’s Eye Diamond.  This won’t be the card that keeps you from completing the deck.  However, the new art is really sweet.  I’m not sure what all is going in that cannon, but it looks like at least a Black Lotus and either a Birds of Paradise or Storm Crow.

 
Constructed Appeal: High
Cube Appeal: Low
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

EMA limited rare.

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: Low
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

This is a powerful option in Legacy.  It can find whatever threat you need for a single additional mana.  Dryad Arbor turns Green Sun’s Zenith into a Rampant Growth for a single mana.  Most fair green decks in Legacy should have a good reason not to run this card.

Constructed Appeal: High
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: High
 
 
 

There is one home for Heritage Druid, and it’s real obvious.  Legacy Elves uses Heritage Druid to generate mana from freshly cast elves.  It is a key part to the deck.

Constructed Appeal: High
Cube Appeal: Low
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

I don’t think there are any fair decks that want Ichorid.  Legacy and Vintage Dredge use Ichorid as a free creature and a way to trigger Bridge from Below and to cast Cabal Therapy.

Constructed Appeal: High – Dredge
Cube Appeal: Low
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

This, like Heritage Druid was shifted from an Uncommon to a Rare.  Unlike Heritage Druid, Imperious Perfect does not have a place in Legacy Elves.  It probably shows up more in Commander though.

Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: Low
Commander Appeal: Medium
 
 
 

This is a most commonly seen as a backup Reanimator target against decks like Miracles and Shardless Sultai.  Really, it is decent against any deck with Islands that you have a few turns to win the game.  Cubes often run Inkwell Leviathan as a Tinker target.

Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: Medium
Commander Appeal: Medium
 
 
 

There really aren’t that many decks that play Isochron Scepter anymore.  There is too much artifact hate already present in every format for it to really shine.  Cubes sometimes play it, but Commander decks often run too few spells that can actually imprint on the Scepter

Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: Medium
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

The man, the myth, the legend himself, Jace, the Mind Sculptor is really only playable in the slowest Legacy decks.  Vintage is a little different, as even the “slow” decks can still get him into play on turn one.  Jace shows up in plenty of cubes.  Commander is probably his weakest format, since he will pull a lot of hate and doesn’t have a particularly useful ability set for multiplayer.

Constructed Appeal: High
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: Medium
 
 
 

I’m not even sure there is a limited deck in EMA that wants this guy.  I guess it’s a Cat for your tribal deck…

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: None
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

This is great against several Legacy decks for the low cost of a single land slot.  The only penalty is making your Plains vulnerable to Wasteland.  There is a ton of upside though, from bouncing their Emrakul or Griselbrand to saving your own Thalia, Vendillion Clique, or Venser. Karakas has earned its price tag in Legacy.  Vintage can’t afford the land slot as often, and the Emrakul threat isn’t as common there.  Cubes sometimes play Karakas, and Commander has banned it.

Constructed Appeal: High
Cube Appeal: Medium
Commander Appeal: None
 
 
 

There are lots of fun interactions with Karmic Guide in both Cube and Commander.  Not so much in Legacy

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: High
 
 
 

Hypergenesis decks will sometimes play Maelstrom Wanderer to give the rest of their threats Haste.  That’s about the extent of Wanderer in Legacy.  Cube and Commander decks can pay the expensive mana cost, and get the huge amount of value Maelstrom Wanderer offers.  It gives up to 22 mana worth of spells for only eight, and gives any creatures you get haste on top of that.  It is one of the best things you can do with eight mana.

Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: High
 
 
 

This originally came out in Commander 2014, and hasn’t made much of a splash in any format since then.  It does give some additional value in Commander, where a single target removal spell is often too inefficient to warrant inclusion in the deck.  It can occasionally see play in Cube and hasn’t really made an impact in Legacy yet.

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: Low
Commander Appeal: Medium
 
 
 

One of the headlining cards in EMA, Mana Crypt is a homerun addition to any Commander deck.  Vintage decks will often play it too, but the Power Nine will probably restrict anyone not already invested in the format.  Powered Cubes will often play Mana Crypt too.

Constructed Appeal: High
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: High
 
 
 

This is a great card in Commander, and I’m never quite sure why the price doesn’t seem to go up.  It has a few reprints, but they aren’t in very big quantities.  This one will probably keep the price down for a long time though.  Legacy Lands plays Maze of Ith.  Commander decks and Cubes will often run it too.

Constructed Appeal: Medium
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: High
 
 
 

This card is an absolute nightmare to play against.  Legacy Death and Taxes (the mono white aggro/prison deck) would have a hard time competing against fair decks if it wasn’t for this card.  It is pretty embarrassing against the unfair decks.  Cubes will often play it, but it is a little low impact for Commander

Constructed Appeal: High
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

This tutor is banned in Legacy, which really reduces the places it can see play.  Vintage plays it so it can find a bunch of broken Restricted cards.  Cube and Commander both play it a fair amount.

Constructed Appeal: Medium
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: Medium
 
 
 

 This is one of the main win conditions in Legacy Elves.  Several other decks used to run a Natural Order package for Progenitus, but that has lost popularity in recent years.  Cubes often include Natural Order.  Commander decks are a little less interested in it.

Constructed Appeal: High
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: Medium
 
 
 

I’m not really sure why we got a reprint of this one.  Copies weren’t that hard to find already, and it really only shows up in Vintage and occasionally in Cubes.  It isn’t exactly a popular Commander card, and it is banned in Legacy.  Oh well, at least it is another way to find a foil copy, which was a more difficult challenge to find.

Constructed Appeal: Medium
Cube Appeal: Medium
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

This is a slow Wrath effect that you can play in any color.  It works great with Planeswalkers, since it skips those.  Constructed formats don’t really touch Disk.  Cubes use it sometimes.  It is a popular option in Commander, since it gives colors like red and black ways to handle Enchantments.

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: Medium
Commander Appeal: High
 
 
 

Pyrokinesis moved up to rare for EMA limited.  It can show up in the sideboard of Legacy Goblins, and that is about it.

Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: None
Commander Appeal: None
 
 
 

This used to be a Natural Order target in Elves before Craterhoof Behemoth took over that spot.  Now, Natural Order can just kill your opponent instead of letting you reload your hand.  Commander decks use it for the card advantage, but Cube doesn’t really touch it.

Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: None
Commander Appeal: Medium
 
 
 

I really dislike that Rorix makes an appearance here.  That was the first rare I ever opened out of a booster pack, and I remember it fondly.  Now, I can crack it in a booster pack, find Rorix Bladewing, and feel miserable that I incinerated $10.

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: None
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

Legacy Miracles pretty much depends on Top to set up Counterbalance.  Most slow Legacy decks that don’t run blue (for Brainstorm) often use Sensei’s Divining Top for come card selection.   Commander decks use it all the time.  Cubes often include it.

Constructed Appeal: High
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: High
 
 
 

Sea Stompy was an old Legacy deck that relied on cheap blue threats.  Time has pretty much passed it by, and Temur Delver has taken any spot that Sea Stompy ever actually had in the Legacy metagame.  Cubes will use Serendib Efreet if they have an aggressive blue section.  I can’t really imagine the Commander deck that wants it.

Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: Low
Commander Appeal: None
 
 
 

This is a great way to get a foil copy of Shardless Agent.  Actually, this is a great way to get regular Shardless Agents if you weren’t playing when the Planechase decks were originally printed.  Shardless Agent is one of the core pieces of the Shardless Sultai deck, cascading into Ancestral Vision to draw three cards immediately.  Cubes will often play it, since it is one of the few good Simic cards available.  Shardless agent is a little too small for most Commander decks

Constructed Appeal: High
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

Good in Legacy Goblins as a Goblin Matron target.  Good in Cube as a midrange threat.  Decent in Commander if you want tokens or an outlet for your Goblins.

Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: Medium
Commander Appeal: Medium
 
 
 

This is for EMA limited as a target for Reanimator and Natural Order.

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: None
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

If you really want to make sure your opponent doesn’t actually get to play Magic.  There aren’t many decks in Legacy that use Sinkhole, but it does show up occasionally.  Cubes often use it.  Commander decks usually think bigger than a single land, and Strip Mine and Wasteland are available if a problem arises.

Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: Medium
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

This is a premier combo piece for the Sneak and Show deck in Legacy.  It is a great way to get into Legacy, ignoring the Volcanic Islands needed.  You can probably play that deck with only a single one if budget is a concern.  Commander and Cubes will both happily use Sneak Attack to put giant creatures into play early.  They have made creatures better over the years as they removed cards like Sneak Attack.  This is likely one of the best that will ever exist.

Constructed Appeal: High
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: High
 
 
 

This is a Reanimator target against red decks.  It can also double as a Tinker target in Cubes.  Most Esper Artifact Commander decks will run Sphinx because of the colors.  None of those things are very common.

Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: Medium
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 
This is a good card in Legacy Burn.  It is also a great card in Cube.  It isn’t really a card to make friends with in Commander
Constructed Appeal: Medium
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

One of the best ways to draw cards outside of Blue, Sylvan Library shows up in small numbers in plenty of different Legacy decks.  It is a solid card in both Cube and Commander.  It is also the source of tons of Judge calls, as it can lead to really weird rule interactions.

Constructed Appeal: High
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: Medium
 
 
 

Deluge is mostly an inferior Wrath of God, but the mana discount is extremely good.  Legacy decks often need the cheaper cost to even keep up, considering that Wasteland and Rishadan Port are common mana denial cards.  It also helps in some corner case scenarios, like with Indestructible creatures. Cubes often play it since it is a solid with or instead of Damnation.  Commander decks don’t mind the life loss as much, and it is a cheaper alternative to Damantion.

Constructed Appeal: Medium
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: Medium
 
 
 

This is a niche Legacy card, without a consistent home.  Cubes often play it, and Commander decks will occasionally consider it.

Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: Medium
 
 
 

Vampiric Tutor is banned in Legacy and very good in Vintage.  It’s great in Cube and it’s great in Commander.  Not getting the card until the following turn is rough, but you can find whatever you need.  It isn’t Demonic Tutor, but it is about as close as you can get.

Constructed Appeal: Medium
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: High
 
 
 

This used to be the best universal answer available.  Times have changed, and cards have gotten better.  Vindicate still shows up occasionally, mostly because it can blow up a land.  Council’s Judgment shows up more now since it can hit True-Name Nemesis and doesn’t need both white and black.  Cube and Commander will both consider it if the can deal with the color requirements.

Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: Medium
 
 
 

This is a great card for EMA limited.  It isn’t really that exciting elsewhere

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: Low
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

I’m betting 99% of Magic players didn’t actually know this card existed.  It was probably better off that way.

Constructed Appeal: None
Cube Appeal: None
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

If your Legacy deck can function off of 1 or two lands, it is probably running Wasteland.  It is the card that keeps manabases in check in Legacy.  Commander decks run it to deal with obnoxious lands that can be difficult to remove.  Cube decks run Wasteland in almost any aggressive deck.

Constructed Appeal: High
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: High
 
 
 

This card is just misery.

Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

Here is your feel bad Mythic for EMA.  It makes infinite mana if you Reanimate it with Animate Dead or Necromancy, which is actually a fringe combo deck in Vintage.  Worldgorger Dragon doesn’t see play anywhere else.

Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: Low
Commander Appeal: Low
 
 
 

This is the classic Wrath effect (hence the name).  This is for when things just need killing.  A little too slow in Legacy compared to Terminus, but great in Cube and Commander.

 Constructed Appeal: Low
Cube Appeal: High
Commander Appeal: High
 
 
 

For when you don’t want to fight fair, and you don’t want your opponents to break up your fun.  Xantid Swarm is a sideboard card in several decks that are weak to counter magic.  It doesn’t really have a place in Cube or Commander

Constructed Appeal: Medium
Cube Appeal: None
Commander Appeal: Low
 

That covers the gold and orange set symbols for EMA.  Don’t forget to pay attention to the uncommons and foils you pick up, as they could be worth a lot more than you expect.
